# ZTTP Domain Rules  
**Version 1.1 — HyperZHub Official Guidelines**

ZTTP is a free, TLD web ecosystem designed for creativity, safety, and long‑term stability.  
These rules ensure the ZTTP universe remains clean, active, and enjoyable for everyone.

---

## 1. ZTTP Is Completely Free
ZTTP domains cost nothing and will always be free.

- No domain fees  
- No renewal costs  
- No DNS registration  
- No expiration timers  
- No charges of any kind

Domains are hosted through the HyperZHub GitHub repository and remain active as long as they follow the rules.

---

## 2. Any `.tld` Is Allowed
ZTTP accepts **any** top‑level domain, including fictional or custom ones such as:

- `.sigma`  
- `.hyperz`  
- `.void`  
- `.matrix`  
- `.org`
- `.anything-you-want`

There are **no ICANN restrictions** and no DNS limitations.  
If it ends in `.something`, ZTTP treats it as valid.

---

## 3. Content Restrictions
To keep the ZTTP ecosystem safe and clean, the following are **not allowed**:

- ❌ 18+ or NSFW content  
- ❌ Malware, viruses, or harmful scripts  
- ❌ Scams, phishing, or deceptive pages  
- ❌ Illegal content of any kind  

Any domain violating these rules will be removed immediately.

---

## 4. Monthly Update Requirement
All ZTTP domains must be updated **at least once every month**.

This ensures the ZTTP universe stays:

- active  
- fresh  
- maintained  
- free of abandoned projects  

Updates can be small — even a minor change counts.

---

## 5. If You Stop Updating, You MUST Notify
If you no longer wish to maintain your ZTTP domain:

### ➜ **You must notify the HyperZHub maintainer.**

This prevents your domain from being removed accidentally for inactivity.

If you notify ahead of time, your domain can be:

- archived  
- preserved  
- marked as inactive  
- or removed cleanly (your choice)

Domains that go silent with no updates **and** no communication will be taken down.

---

## 6. Submission Process
To publish a ZTTP domain:

1. Create your site (One HTML file main site, then other html files for / domains)
2. Email the source code to the HyperZHub maintainer  
3. Your domain will be added to this repo  
4. HyperZ will fetch and serve it automatically

This curated process ensures quality, safety, and stability across the entire ZTTP network.

---

## 7. Transparency & Versioning
All ZTTP domains are stored in a public GitHub repository for:

- version control  
- transparency  
- backups  
- easy updates  
- community trust  

---

## 8. HTTP/HTTPS Compatibility
HyperZ supports both:

- standard HTTP/HTTPS websites  
- ZTTP TLD domains  

Both coexist seamlessly inside the HyperZHub browser.

---

## 9. Purpose of ZTTP
ZTTP exists to create a **clean, safe, creative, and free fictional‑TLD web**, free from:

- DNS bureaucracy  
- ICANN restrictions  
- renewal fees  
- corporate tracking  
- abandoned junk domains  

It is a curated multiverse of domains built for creativity and long‑term stability.
